The City of Englewood, nestled within Arapahoe County, Colorado, stands as a dynamic home rule municipality that boasts a rich history and a vibrant present. This town, whose population reached 33,659 according to the 2020 United States Census, finds its place in the Denver–Aurora–Lakewood, CO Metropolitan Statistical Area and the Front Range Urban Corridor. Positioned just to the south of Denver, Englewood's scenic backdrop is the picturesque South Platte River Valley.

The origins of Englewood trace back to the Gold Rush era of 1858, ignited by William Green Russell's discovery of gold along Little Dry Creek. By 1860, Thomas Skerritt, hailed as the city's founder, established his abode in an area called Orchard Place, which he connected to Denver with the region's first road. The landscape soon saw the emergence of vital infrastructure, including the Cherrelyn horsecar path in 1883 and the introduction of the telephone in 1879.

Significant milestones pepper Englewood's history. The city was formally incorporated in 1903, though the inaugural mayorship went to J.C. Jones over Skerritt. The iconic Cherrelyn trolley, hauled by horses up Broadway and down by gravity, became a city symbol.
